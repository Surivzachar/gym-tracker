## 🎯 TRAINING PHILOSOPHY

**Goal:** Get to 10-12% body fat while preserving muscle

**Strategy:**
- Moderate volume (not too high during calorie deficit)
- Compound movements priority
- Progressive overload (add weight when possible)
- Daily abs (reveal that six-pack)
- 2 cardio days (fat burning)

**Training Days:** 6 days/week + 1 rest day (optional light activity)

## 📈 PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD PROTOCOL

**Weeks 1-4: Establish Baseline**
- Learn proper form for all exercises
- Record all weights in your app
- Focus: Consistency, technique

**Weeks 5-8: Progressive Overload**
- When you hit the top rep range easily (e.g., 4x10), add weight next session
- Increase by smallest increment: 2.5kg for upper body, 5kg for lower body
- If you can't complete all reps with new weight, that's OK - work up to it

**Weeks 9-12: Intensity Techniques**
- Drop sets: On last set, reduce weight by 20-30% and do more reps to failure
- Rest-pause: On last set, go to failure, rest 15 seconds, go to failure again
- Slow negatives: 3-4 second lowering phase on last set

## 💡 TRAINING TIPS FOR FAT LOSS

### 1. Rest Periods
- **Compound movements:** 90-120 seconds (squats, deadlifts, bench press)
- **Isolation movements:** 45-60 seconds (curls, lateral raises, leg extensions)
- **Abs/Core:** 30-60 seconds

### 2. Tempo
- **2-1-2 tempo:** 2 seconds down, 1 second pause, 2 seconds up
- Control the weight, don't use momentum

### 3. Form Over Ego
- Perfect form > heavy weight
- During calorie deficit, your strength may plateau - that's normal
- Goal is to MAINTAIN strength, not necessarily gain

### 4. Track Everything
- Use your app to log all exercises, sets, reps, weight
- This is how you know if you're progressing

### 5. Listen to Your Body
- Feeling fatigued? Reduce volume slightly
- Joint pain? Check form, reduce weight
- Can't recover? Ensure you're eating enough protein

## ⚡ IMPORTANT NOTES

### During 12-Week Fat Loss Phase:

✅ **DO:**
- Maintain current weights/reps as much as possible
- Focus on muscle preservation, not muscle gain
- Prioritize compound movements
- Track all workouts in app
- Get 7-8 hours sleep for recovery

❌ **DON'T:**
- Expect to gain strength every week (you're in a deficit)
- Go to absolute failure on every set (recovery is harder in deficit)
- Add extra volume (more is not better when cutting)
- Skip rest days (recovery is crucial)
- Train through pain (injury will derail your progress)

### Strength During Cut:

**Expected:**
- Weeks 1-4: Maintain or slight increase (newbie gains + motivation)
- Weeks 5-8: Maintain strength (this is success!)
- Weeks 9-12: Slight decrease is OK (you're very lean now, less glycogen)

**If strength drops significantly:**
- Check if protein is 160g+ daily
- Check if you're eating enough (minimum 1,950 kcal)
- Check if sleep is 7-8 hours
- Reduce cardio volume if needed

## ❓ COMMON QUESTIONS

**Q: Can I swap exercises?**
A: Yes, but keep similar movement patterns:
- Bench press → Dumbbell press ✅
- Squats → Leg press ❌ (squats are superior)

**Q: What if I can't do pull-ups?**
A: Use lat pulldown machine or assisted pull-up machine. Work towards unassisted!

**Q: My gym doesn't have a cable machine.**
A: Substitute with dumbbells or resistance bands for similar movements.

**Q: Do I need to do abs every day?**
A: YES! For visible abs at 10-12% body fat, high ab frequency helps. Plus you're in a deficit, so recovery is fine.

**Q: Can I add more exercises?**
A: NO! This volume is appropriate for fat loss. More volume = harder recovery in a deficit.

**Q: What if I miss a workout?**
A: Resume next day with the next scheduled workout. Don't try to make it up or double up.
